Can the computer generate polygons?

Details

Experiments with p5.js during the Xperimenter Programme at Science Gallery Bengaluru

Role

Independent Experiment

People

Random professor at NCBS for the inspiration

Thought

During my time at NCBS as part of the Xperimenters programme at Science Gallery Bengaluru, I wandered around the campus a lot. Being around _smart_ people has always fascinated me, for I like to find my own tiny meaning in their gargantuan complexity. What struck me one day was a whiteboard outside the main academic building, possibly something that the professors used to explain concepts outdoors.

The whiteboard presented an interesting visual of a shape being formed by joining dots on the circumference of a circle. I have no idea about the meaning of this shape or the complex jargon that surrounded it. The idea, however, of lines originating from a point to join all the other points was visually interesting.


I wondered what kind of shapes could be generated by a computer that could move around interconnected dots. Wrote a program on p5.js to simulate just that.
