How can we make young adults question human needs?

Details

16-week long project with Science Gallery Bengaluru to program a web game that prompts young adults to question human needs

Exhibited in the PSYCHE exhibition, adjudged Best Graduation Project at IIAD and part of the p5.js Global Showcase 2022

Role

Self-directed

Mentors

Shaunaq, Saibal, Shashi, Ramanujam, Madhushree & Vasudha

Context

Every final semester student at the Indian Institute of Art & Design is expected to undertake an independently driven 16 week long graduation project. This is meant to conclude their 4-year program, by resulting in a substantial response to an individually defined design issue of interest.

During my final semester at IIAD, I was selected by Science Gallery Bengaluru to be a part of their maiden Xperimenters cohort: a 6-month long programme to offer interdisciplinary mentorship for 4 young adults to drive public engagement projects at the gallery.


A screengrab of the Xperimenters page on Science Gallery Bengaluru's website in 2022.

One of the main projects for each Xperimenter was to independently conceptualise, plan and execute a public engagement programme for PSYCHE: an online exhibition that explored the human mind. This became my design brief for the graduation project as well.

By carefully merging the professional requirements of creating a public engagement programme & following a human-centred project philosophy, I developed In Between Life & Death: a web-based game that converted A.H Maslow’s Hierarchy of Human Needs into playable levels. A player wakes up as a digital particle and attempts to progress through the hierarchies by fulfilling the requirements of each. The varied experiences of young adults around the country who played the game were later critically discussed in Breaking Down The Making: an online workshop where participants questioned my interpretation of the theory, human needs, happiness and the existence (or inexistence) of a grand life purpose.
